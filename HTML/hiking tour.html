Hike To Adems Peak(Two day)

Make the trip to Adam's Peak and take in the breathtaking sunrise views across Sri Lanka's beautiful hill region. When you join us on this trip, you will face the heights of Adam's Peak, an important place of devotion. For ages, this towering mountain has piqued people's interest and served as a pilgrimage destination for more than 1000 years. Buddhists in Sri Lanka believe that it houses the holy imprint of Gautama Buddha, as well as the adobe of the deity "Sumana Saman," who guards the sacred footprint of Gautama Buddha. This challenging hiking tour will take you up Adam's Peak's most revered 5500 stairs.You can participate in this hiking excursion during the season or during the off-season. If you travel during the summer, there's a good possibility you'll see pilgrims performing centuries-old rites associated with Adam's Peak's past. In Sri Lanka, this is a must-see location!

Hiking Horton Plains National Park

Horton Plains in Sri Lanka is the country's coldest and windiest region. Montane evergreen forests, grasslands, marshy areas, and aquatic habitats are among the ecosystems found there. Horton Plains, at an elevation of 2,100 meters above sea level, covers 3,169 hectares of the island's highest tableland. Horton Plains was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site on July 30, 2010, due to its vast number of unique plant and animal species.Discover the Horton Plains National Park, a protected region in Sri Lanka's central highlands, and its remarkable biodiversity and abundant flora. After taking a picturesque train journey through the stunning scenery, stroll to the World's End Viewpoint.Horton Plains has the climate of a moist montane forest. The average yearly temperature is 14-160 degrees Celsius, with a low humidity of 65 percent. Although the highlands receive around 2540 mm of yearly rainfall, Horton Plains receives about 5000 mm.

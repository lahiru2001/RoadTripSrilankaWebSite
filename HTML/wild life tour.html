Wilpattu National Park (wild life safari)

Wilpattu Safari is the greatest site in Sri Lanka to see leopards and a diverse range of animals. Apart from the animals, your safari will provide you with plenty of opportunities to see wonderfully gorgeous wetlands of butterflies, such as the great egg fly, great orange tip, glad-eye bushbrown, blue Mormon, common Mormon, crimson rose, and others. Catch a variety of animals on your safari thanks to the gorgeous coastal belt, small lakes, extensive scrublands, treeless plains, and thick woods. The tiny water features strewn around the park are a significant element that add to the area's picture appeal while also drawing visitors.

Sri Lanka's state religion is Theravada Buddhism, which is practiced by 70.2 percent of the country's population. Sri Lankan kings have played a key role in the preservation and restoration of the island's Buddhist institutions for much of its history.

It is a kaleidoscope of natural wonders, with a diverse ecology that has transformed the park into a wonderful home for a variety of flora and wildlife.

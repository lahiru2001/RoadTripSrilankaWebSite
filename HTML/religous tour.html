Embekke / Lankathilaka & Gadaladeniya One Day Tour

Three famous Buddhist temples, Embekke, Lankatilaka, and Gadaladeniya, are located in Sri Lanka's historic city of Gampola. Each legendary temple is only a short distance apart and is typically visited in order by curious visitors, known as the "three temple loop." The three temples are well worth a visit because of their superb workmanship and historical significance to Sri Lanka's Gampola era. The three temple loop will immerse you in history while also exposing you to amazing architecture. Your experienced guide will explain the origins of Buddhism in Sri Lanka, as well as the role it plays now in the lives of 75 percent of the population.

Day Trip To Kandy & Ambuluwawa Temple

Kandy, which is surrounded by the country's central highlands, is a city rich in history and traditional culture. The city is home to numerous monuments that represent the culture of Sri Lanka. It was the final capital of the ancient rulers of Sri Lanka before the nation fell under British rule. The Temple of the Tooth, also known as Sri Dalada Maligawa, is the most holy Buddhist shrine in the country and serves as the city's center point. The temple, which was formerly a part of the Royal Palace, now holds a relic of Lord Buddha's tooth. The tooth temple in Kandy is claimed to be the latest of several temples erected to hold the relic, which is the major emblem of the Sri Lankan royalty.The visit to Ambuluwawa Temple is the tour's main attraction, and the surrounding sights will undoubtedly leave you awestruck!

Buddhist pilgrimage Tour (5 Day)

Sri Lanka's state religion is Theravada Buddhism, which is practiced by 70.2 percent of the country's population. Since the advent of Buddhism in the third century BCE, the island has been a center of Buddhist research and learning, producing renowned academics like as Buddhaghosa and maintaining the enormous Pli Canon. Sri Lankan kings have played a key role in the preservation and restoration of the island's Buddhist institutions for much of its history.

Anuradahapura Tour(3 day)

Anuradhapura is Sri Lanka's first capital, located in the north central region. It is one of Sri Lanka's historic capitals, and for many years, it was the epicenter of Theravada Buddhism. The Sacred City of Anuradhapura was designated as a UNESCO world heritage site in 1982 due to the remnants of an ancient Sri Lankan civilisation.Anuradhapura, the world's greatest monastic city, originates from the 5th century BC and served as the stately seat of the kingdom of Sri Lanka until the 11th century A.D. From 377 BC to the end of the rule in 1017 AD, Anuradhapura prospered as the city of Lanka, serving as the Royal Seat for 1400 years under 130 monarchs.
